# Stage 1: Desk Research

Part of [the process](process.md).

## What this step was

Not every clause of the [positioning statement](stage-positioning.md)
needs a live conversation to check. Some questions — does this audience
exist, is this category real, is the name already taken, who else is
building this — can be answered faster and more objectively with desk
research: app-store categories, competitor marketing, roundup articles,
and product listings. The research plan named which clauses to check this
way, what would count as "enough" evidence to stop, and what the student
expected to find going in — so a surprising result would actually stand
out as surprising.

## Results

- **Target customer & need:** confirmed as real and reachable — a whole
  product category (Howbout, Flaky, Hangs, Partiful, GroupMe, and others)
  already targets friend groups stuck in exactly this kind of
  coordination pain.
- **Product name:** a name collision. An existing app already called
  "Link Up" does nearly the same thing (plus a separate "Link Up
  Calendar"). Decision: keep "Link Up" as the working name for now,
  change it before any real launch.
- **Product category:** "group scheduling app" / "shared calendar app" is
  an established, recognized category with ranked players and "best of"
  roundups — Link Up doesn't need to invent a category, just compete in
  one people already search for.
- **Primary competitive alternative:** the group text (WhatsApp/iMessage)
  really is the default, but there's also a crowded field of dedicated
  competitors — Doodle (manual poll/vote), Partiful (event pages once a
  plan exists), Howbout (closest positioning match, still manual).
- **Primary differentiation:** the open niche held up. AI calendar
  assistants exist (Clockwise, Dola, SkedPal, Morgen), but they're
  personal/work-calendar tools. No mainstream product does "AI proposes a
  *group social* plan from availability plus activity preferences" — that
  specific niche was, and remains, open.

Key benefit was deliberately left off this list — the plan named it as a
question only a real conversation could answer, not desk research. See
[Interviews](stage-interviews.md) for that.
