# Stage 2: Discovery Analysis

Part of [the process](process.md).

## What this step was

With the positioning statement holding up under Stage 1's evidence, Stage
2 zoomed out: who else is in this space, who has a stake in the system
beyond the people using it, and what are the concrete jobs people are
hiring this product to do — described as scenarios specific enough to
walk through later, in the wireframes. The positioning statement gets
checked again here too, as a second pressure test — if anything in this
analysis contradicts a clause, the clause changes.

## Results

- **Landscape.** Surveyed six adjacent products (Howbout, Doodle,
  Partiful, GroupMe/group text, When2meet/WhenAvailable, and the existing
  "Link Up" app) plus two open-source references (Rallly, Timeful/schej.it)
  for conventions worth studying. Decided which conventions to keep —
  no-account invite links, calendar sync, an RSVP/event page — and which
  to deliberately break: the manual availability grid and the manual
  "finalize the date" step, since removing exactly that manual work is
  Link Up's differentiation.
- **Stakeholders.** Beyond the user, four stakeholders were named: app
  store platforms, calendar API providers (Google/Apple), the underlying
  AI provider, and — explicitly — friends who get pulled into a plan
  without ever installing the app themselves.
- **Compliance and accessibility.** Calendar data was flagged as
  sensitive by nature, not a peripheral concern. COPPA was confirmed in
  scope (under-13 users are plausible), which carries real parental
  consent and data-handling obligations into Stage 3 as an open item.
- **Jobs-to-be-done.** Two jobs, each with a full scenario and a "something
  goes wrong" branch: (1) getting a hangout to actually come together
  despite a last-minute schedule change, and (2) getting an event planned
  at all, when past attempts fizzled before they even started — including
  the case of a friend who never installed the app.
- **Positioning statement revisited:** no revision needed. Stage 2
  confirmed the Stage 1 statement rather than contradicting it.
