# UA3 — Research Plan

Produced in Pass 2 of Stage 1. Names what desk research the student is going to do, in service of which clauses of the positioning statement, with what stopping criteria. Updated as research is conducted to log what was searched, what was found, and how the student interpreted it.

Not every clause needs desk research. Sections below are filled in only for clauses that genuinely benefit from outside evidence. Clauses that will be answered by interviews are marked as such.

---

## Target customer

- **Current claim:** Friend groups with busy, clashing schedules.
- **Questions:** Is "friend groups trying to make social plans" a real, reachable audience, or a niche?
- **Sources:** App-store categories, market roundups.
- **Enough:** Light — the deepest validation of who the customer is comes from interviews, not desk research.
- **Expected findings:** It's a mass-market audience.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-06-27 → A whole product category exists targeting exactly this audience (Howbout, Flaky, Hangs, Partiful, GroupMe, etc.), reachable via app stores. **Interpretation:** the audience is real and reachable; the *specific human* and how acute their pain is must come from interviews.

---

## Need or opportunity

- **Current claim:** They want to make plans together but get stuck in the back-and-forth until the plan falls apart.
- **Questions:** Is "group chats that never turn into plans" a pain other people recognize, not just me?
- **Sources:** Competitor marketing, app roundups.
- **Enough:** A few independent sources naming the pain.
- **Expected findings:** Others feel it too.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-06-27 → Multiple apps market against this exact pain — Howbout and the existing "Link Up" both pitch "group chats that never turn into plans." **Interpretation:** the need is real and widely recognized enough that companies build around it. (Strong supporting signal; the *intensity* — does it kill plans? — still best tested in interviews.)

## Product name

- **Current claim:** Link Up.
- **Questions:** Does an existing app already use the name "Link Up" for this kind of product?
- **Enough:** A single app-store / web search.
- **Expected findings:** Maybe a minor name collision, nothing serious.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-06-27, search `"Link Up" app scheduling friends` → **There is already an app called "Link Up"** doing almost exactly this: "organise real-life activities... addresses group chats that never turn into plans" (App Store id6758226034; Google Play com.linkupmeet). A separate "Link Up Calendar" (shared calendar) also exists. **Interpretation:** [awaiting student's call] — the name is taken by a near-identical product; likely needs to change.

## Product category

- **Current claim:** A group scheduling app.
- **Questions:** Is "group scheduling app" / "shared calendar" an established category people search in?
- **Sources:** Roundup articles, app-store categories.
- **Enough:** Confirm the category exists with recognized players.
- **Expected findings:** It's an established category.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-06-27 → "Group scheduling tools" / "shared calendar apps" are well-established categories with many ranked players and "best of 2026" roundups (Zapier, usecarly). **Interpretation:** the category is real and recognized; Link Up doesn't have to invent a category, just compete in one.

## Primary competitive alternative

- **Current claim:** Coordinating by hand in a group text (WhatsApp / iMessage).
- **Questions:** What apps/tools already exist for getting a group to agree on a plan? What do their users complain about?
- **Sources:** App store listings & reviews, Product Hunt, roundup/comparison blogs, competitor sites.
- **Enough:** Stop once the main existing tools are identified across at least 3 independent sources.
- **Expected findings (student's prediction):** Existing tools mostly make groups vote on times manually; the group text is the real default; not much serious competition.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-06-27, search `best apps for scheduling group hangouts with friends 2026` → A crowded category: Flaky, Howbout, Hangs, Partiful, GroupMe, Doodle, When2meet, WhenAvailable, Grooop. **Howbout** is positioned almost identically to Link Up ("shared calendar for making plans with friends," fixes "group chats that never turn into plans"). (flaky-app.com roundup; howbout.app). **Interpretation:** [awaiting student's call] — the group text is the default, but there is also a real field of dedicated competitors, and at least one (Howbout) targets this exact pain.
  - 2026-06-27, search `Howbout vs Doodle vs Partiful ... complaints` → Doodle = manual poll/vote, "not designed for automation"; Partiful = event pages/invites (complaints: privacy, over-the-top design, character limits); Howbout = shared availability calendar (sparsely reviewed). (producthunt.com; cal.com; usecarly.com). **Interpretation:** [awaiting student's call] — existing tools still put the coordination work on the people (vote, poll, manually check); none described as "AI proposes the plan."

## Primary differentiation

- **Current claim:** Uses an AI assistant to juggle everyone's availability and preferences and propose a plan that works.
- **Questions:** Does any existing tool already use AI to propose a group social plan from everyone's availability + activity preferences?
- **Sources:** AI-scheduling roundups, product sites, app reviews.
- **Enough:** Stop once it's clear whether a mainstream tool does AI group-plan proposal.
- **Expected findings (student's prediction):** Nothing does the "AI just proposes a plan" part well; it's open space and the real selling point.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-06-27, search `AI app that automatically suggests plans based on group availability and preferences` → AI scheduling tools exist (SkedPal, Dola, Clockwise, Morgen) but they are mostly **personal / work-calendar** assistants. WhenAvailable does group polling but not AI proposal. No mainstream tool clearly does "AI proposes a group *social* plan from everyone's availability + activity preferences." (morgen.so; retellai.com; whenavailable.com). **Interpretation:** [awaiting student's call] — the AI part is more open than the category overall, but AI calendar assistants do exist; the open niche is specifically *group social* plan proposal, not "AI scheduling" in general.
