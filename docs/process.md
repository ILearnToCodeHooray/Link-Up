# Our Process

Link Up wasn't designed by guessing. It came out of a structured,
evidence-driven research process (the [`ua-framework`][ua]) that forces
every claim about the product — who it's for, what problem it solves, why
it's different — to be either backed by evidence or flagged as an open
risk. Nothing gets marked "done" until it's been stress-tested against
real desk research or a real conversation with a real person.

[ua]: https://github.com/league-infrastructure/claude-plugin-marketplace/tree/master/plugins/ua-framework

The process ran in four stages, each producing a concrete document, ending
with a set of navigable wireframes and — beyond the framework itself — a
working prototype:

1. **[Opportunity discovery](stage-opportunity.md)** — start from a real,
   personally-experienced problem, not an idea. Look for the pattern
   underneath it.
2. **Stage 1 — Positioning & validation**
   - **[Positioning statement](stage-positioning.md)** — commit to a
     one-paragraph claim about who the customer is, what they need, and
     why this product beats the alternative.
   - **[Desk research](stage-research.md)** — check the claims that
     research can check: does the audience exist, is the category real,
     does the name collide with anything, who else is already trying to
     solve this.
   - **[Interviews](stage-interviews.md)** — check the claims only a real
     person can check, with falsification commitments written down
     *before* the conversation happens, not after.
3. **[Stage 2 — Discovery analysis](stage-discovery-analysis.md)** — map
   the competitive landscape, name the stakeholders beyond the user,
   and write out the jobs-to-be-done with concrete scenarios.
4. **Stage 3 — Specification & wireframes**
   - **[Specification](stage-specification.md)** — turn everything above
     into a screen-by-screen spec a developer can build from.
   - **[Wireframes](stage-wireframes.md)** — build it low-fidelity and
     walk real scenarios through it to catch what the spec alone missed.
5. **[Build](stage-build.md)** — outside the framework itself, but the
   natural next step: a working prototype built from the spec and
   wireframes.

At every stage, the positioning statement gets revisited. If new evidence
contradicts a clause, the clause changes — the statement is a living
document, not something written once and defended forever. See the
[project status log](UA0-PROJECT-STATUS.md) for the full checkpoint
history, or jump straight to any stage above for what that step actually
produced.
